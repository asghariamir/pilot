Two Ways to Analyze Growth

🔢 Using Differences

Look at the Finite Differences table above. For polynomials, repeated differences eventually become constant (x² reaches constant at Δ²f, x³ at Δ³f). For exponentials, differences keep doubling.

📊 Using Ratios

Look at the Ratio columns in the comparison table. For 2ˣ, each value is exactly 2 times the previous (ratio = 2.00). For x², the ratio decreases: 4.00, 2.25, 1.78... approaching 1.

Key Insight: These two analysis methods reveal the fundamental difference: exponentials have constant multiplicative growth (same ratio), while polynomials have changing additive growth (differences that stabilize).
